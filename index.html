The Problem Space

What are we listening to?

Ann Arbor is home to a diverse ecosystem of urban wildlife. By leveraging foundational Digital Signal Processing (DSP) techniques, this project aims to isolate, analyze, and identify the calls of local animals—such as squirrels, raccoons, and indigenous birds—from the chaotic background noise of a busy college town.

Our Pipeline

Methods & Data

1. Data Collection

Audio recordings were taken across various locations including the Diag and Nichols Arboretum using standard microphones at a 44.1kHz sampling rate.

2. Noise Filtering

Applied high-pass and band-stop filters to remove low-frequency wind, traffic rumble, and standard 60Hz electrical interference.

3. FFT & Spectrograms

Converted the time-domain signals into the frequency domain using Short-Time Fourier Transforms (STFT) to visualize distinct animal calls.

4. Feature Classification

Extracted peak frequencies and harmonic distributions to classify the isolated signals against a database of known animal noises.
